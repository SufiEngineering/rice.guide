Rice Milling 101

Rice milling is the process of removing the outer husk, germ, and bran layers from the rice grain, leaving behind a polished white rice grain. In this guide, we will discuss the basics of rice milling and the different types of rice grains.

Introduction

Rice milling is a crucial step in producing high-quality rice grains that are ready to be cooked and consumed. The process involves removing the outer layers of the rice grain, including the husk, germ, and bran, to produce polished white rice kernels that are free from impurities and broken kernels. The success of the milling process is dependent on various factors, such as the quality of the paddy, the equipment used, and the expertise of the mill operator. A well-executed rice milling system ensures that the final product is not only visually appealing but also meets the required quality standards. In this rice milling guide, we will explore the essential aspects of rice milling, including the different types of rice grains, the milling process, equipment used, and quality control measures.

Rice Grain

Physical Characteristics

The outermost layer of the rice grain is the hull, which is a protective covering that is inedible to humans. Underneath the hull, the bran layer contains high levels of fiber and nutrients, including vitamins and minerals. The bran layer gives the rice grain its characteristic brown color and slightly nutty flavor. The germ layer is the next layer, located just beneath the bran, and contains healthy oils and essential nutrients. The endosperm layer, located at the center of the rice grain, contains most of the starch and provides the blue source of carbohydrates for the grain.

During the rice milling process, the outermost layers of the rice grain, including the hull, bran, and germ, are removed, leaving behind the white endosperm layer. This process transforms brown rice into white rice, which is a staple food in many parts of the world.

Chemical Characteristics

Rice is primarily composed of carbohydrates, but it also contains small amounts of protein, fats, and essential minerals. Carbohydrates make up the largest percentage of the rice grain's composition, with the endosperm layer containing about 80% starch. Starch is a complex carbohydrate that provides energy to the body, and it is the blue source of carbohydrates in the human diet. Rice grain also contains small amounts of protein, with the bran layer containing higher levels than the endosperm. The protein in rice grain is not considered a complete protein as it lacks some essential amino acids, but it is still an important source of protein in many diets.

The fat content of rice grain is relatively low, with the germ layer containing the highest levels. The fats in rice grain are primarily unsaturated, making them beneficial for heart health. Rice grain also contains essential minerals such as iron, magnesium, and potassium. These minerals play important roles in maintaining healthy bodily functions, including regulating blood pressure and supporting muscle and nerve function.
